Masinop ("Masinop", "we", "us", or "our") is a personal finance and expense-tracking application built around a four-bucket budgeting system (Needs · Wants · Debt · Savings). This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect, how we use and protect it, who we share it with, and the rights you have over your information. We wrote it to be clear and honest — not just to tick a legal box.

By creating a Masinop account and using the app, you agree to the practices described in this policy.

1. Summary (the short version)

  • You are in control. Masinop is designed to be offline-first. Your financial records live on your device and sync to your private, secured cloud account so you can restore them and use the app across sessions.
  • We never sell your data. We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal or financial information to anyone, and we do not use it for third-party advertising.
  • Sensitive local data is encrypted. Your App Lock PIN is never stored; we keep only a salted SHA-256 hash inside the device's hardware-backed secure store. Your login token is stored the same encrypted way.
  • Clipboard parsing happens strictly on your device. When Masinop offers to import a bank/e-wallet notification you copied, that text is read and parsed locally, never uploaded, and nothing is saved until you confirm it.
  • Receipt scanning is the one feature that uploads content. If you use the AI Receipt Scanner, the image you choose is sent to our secure backend for OCR and categorization. This only happens when you deliberately scan a receipt.

The sections below explain each point in full.

2. Information we collect

2.1 Information you provide

  • Account details: your display name, email address, and your chosen base currency when you register.
  • Authentication: a password you set. We never store your password in plain text — it is salted and hashed on our server before storage (see Section 6).
  • Financial records you enter: accounts, balances, transactions, budget categories and bucket allocations, savings goals, debts, investments, and recurring bills.
  • Receipt images (optional): only the specific images you choose to scan with the AI Receipt Scanner.

2.2 Information created as you use the app

  • App-generated financial data: dashboard aggregates, net-worth history, monthly reports, and your Financial Health score, all derived from the records above.
  • Operational/technical data: minimal data needed to keep the service working and secure — for example authentication tokens, request timestamps, and rate-limiting counters (used to block brute-force login attempts and to protect the AI scanning quota). We do not build advertising profiles.

2.3 Information we do not collect

  • We do not read your SMS messages. Masinop uses a user-initiated clipboard import ("Shared Clipboard Loop") instead of a background SMS listener — see Section 4.
  • We do not access your contacts, call logs, photos library (beyond the single image you pick to scan), or precise location.
  • We do not continuously monitor your clipboard in the background.

4. The "Shared Clipboard Loop" (on-device only)

Masinop can help you log a transaction from a bank or e-wallet notification (for example a GCash, Maya, BPI, or BDO alert) that you copied to your clipboard. Because this is a sensitive feature, we want to be completely transparent:

  • On-device parsing only. When you open or return to the app, Masinop may check the clipboard for text that looks like a financial notification. That text is analyzed entirely on your device by a local parser. It is never sent to our servers or any third party.
  • Nothing is saved automatically. If the text looks like a transaction, Masinop pre-fills the manual transaction form and asks you to review and confirm. No transaction is created unless you tap to save it.
  • No repeated nagging. The same clipboard text is only offered once, and you can always decline.
  • Fails safe. If the clipboard is empty or unavailable, Masinop simply does nothing — it will never crash or force an import.

In short: clipboard content is treated as a convenience hint that stays on your device and always requires your explicit confirmation before it becomes data.

5. The AI Receipt Scanner (what leaves your device)

The AI Receipt Scanner is the one feature that uploads content you provide:

  • When you scan a receipt, the image you selected is sent over an encrypted (HTTPS/TLS) connection to Masinop's secure backend, where it is processed with Optical Character Recognition (OCR) and a Large Language Model (LLM) to extract line items and sort them into your budget buckets.
  • Processing is tied to your authenticated account and is used only to produce the draft receipt you then review and confirm.
  • You always review and edit the extracted items before anything is posted to your ledger.
  • This upload happens only when you deliberately scan a receipt. If you never use the scanner, no images are ever uploaded.

We use reputable cloud AI processing to power this feature. These providers act as our processors under contract and are not permitted to use your content for their own purposes.

6. How we protect your data

Security is a core design principle of Masinop, not an afterthought.

6.1 On your device

  • App Lock PIN — never stored. If you enable App Lock, we do not save your PIN. We generate a random 16-byte salt and store only the salted SHA-256 hash of your PIN. Verification is done with a constant-time comparison so the stored hash can't be brute-forced by timing.
  • Hardware-backed secure storage. The PIN hash/salt and your login tokens are kept in the platform's encrypted secure store — EncryptedSharedPreferences (backed by the Android Keystore) on Android and the Keychain on iOS — not in ordinary app files.
  • Optional biometrics. Fingerprint/face unlock is handled by the Android system; Masinop never sees or stores your biometric data.
  • Privacy screen. You can hide balances on screen, and this preference is remembered locally so sensitive numbers stay masked from the moment the app opens.

6.2 In transit and in the cloud

  • Encrypted transport. All communication between the app and our backend uses HTTPS/TLS.
  • Secure authentication (JWT). After you log in, the server issues a JSON Web Token (JWT) with a short lifetime plus a rotating refresh token. These tokens are stored in the device's encrypted secure store and are sent only to authenticate your own requests. Every record you access is scoped to your user account on the server.
  • Password hashing. Account passwords are salted and hashed on the server before storage; we never keep them in readable form.
  • Abuse protection. Login and registration endpoints are rate-limited to resist automated brute-force attacks, and the AI scanner is quota-limited per user to keep the service fair and available.

No method of electronic storage or transmission is 100% secure, but we apply these industry-standard safeguards to protect your information.
